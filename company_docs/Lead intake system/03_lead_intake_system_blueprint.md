# Holy Hauling Lead Intake System Blueprint
**Version:** Working Blueprint for Internal Use

**Purpose:** Define the operating model for how Holy Hauling handles new leads using AI, fast contact, call priority, gate-based control, and hospitality-forward communication.

---

## 1. What This System Is

Holy Hauling's lead intake system is an **AI-guided, call-priority, hospitality-forward lead handling process with gate-based control**.

This means:
- AI helps interpret and structure the lead before action
- The first message supports fast human contact
- The phone call is the main qualification and conversion tool for viable leads
- Gates prevent sloppy quoting and overcommitting
- Escalation protects the business from unnecessary risk
- Tone and hospitality help us stand out without becoming loose or overly accommodating

> This system is designed to help Holy Hauling book **better jobs**, not just generate more conversations.

---

## 2. What the System Is Trying to Accomplish

The goal is not to respond to every lead the same way. The goal is to:
- Respond quickly
- Make fast human contact
- Build trust early
- Qualify efficiently
- Protect margin
- Avoid wasting time on weak leads
- Move strong leads toward booking
- Maintain a consistent customer experience

### Success Is Measured By:
- Speed to contact
- Number of meaningful live conversations
- Better-qualified opportunities
- Profitable bookings
- Cleaner release of bad-fit leads
- Fewer pricing mistakes
- Fewer operational surprises

---

## 3. Core Operating Philosophy

This system is built on five major beliefs.

### A. Speed Matters
The faster we make contact, the better our odds of controlling the conversation before competitors do.

### B. Calls Outperform Long Text Threads for Viable Leads
Calls help us build trust, clarify scope, uncover risk, and move toward commitment faster than extended app messaging.

### C. Not Every Lead Deserves the Same Effort
Some leads deserve immediate active pursuit. Some deserve minimal effort. Some should be released quickly.

### D. Pricing Should Stay Controlled
We do not throw out hard numbers too early, especially when scope, access, disposal, or logistics are still unclear.

### E. Hospitality Is a Strategic Advantage
Customers should feel helped, noticed, and reassured. Our tone should reduce stress and make the process feel easier — without weakening our boundaries.

---

## 4. The Role of AI in the System

AI is built into the lead handling process as an **operating tool**, not a side feature. Before action is taken on most viable leads, the lead should be run through the AI workflow.

The AI's role is to help the lead handler:
- Understand the lead quickly
- Identify urgency and risk
- Decide whether a call should happen now
- Determine what matters most to confirm next
- Generate the next customer-facing message
- Prepare for the phone call
- Think about pricing correctly without sending it too early
- Identify when escalation may be needed

> AI is meant to support consistency, speed, and discipline. AI does not replace judgment on risky, unclear, or high-stakes leads.

---

## 5. The Role of the Human Lead Handler

The lead handler is not simply answering messages. The lead handler is an **AI-assisted lead coordinator**.

That role includes:
- Reviewing incoming leads
- Running leads through AI
- Sending first contact
- Attempting calls when appropriate
- Using text and app messaging to support momentum
- Gathering the right details at the right time
- Following gate logic
- Escalating risk instead of freestyling it
- Logging outcomes
- Helping good leads move toward booking

> The lead handler is expected to execute the system, not invent a new one on the fly.

---

## 6. The Lead Flow

> **Lead comes in → AI review → Gate 0 triage → first app reply → call attempt when justified → missed-call SMS if needed → contact and basic qualification → Gate 2A alignment → Gate 2B commitment → escalate if needed → book or release → log and handoff**

This is the backbone of the system. Each stage has a purpose.

| Stage | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Lead comes in** | Gather screenshot, thread, photos, requested timing, any obvious notes |
| **AI review** | Run the lead through the AI structure before acting on most viable leads |
| **Gate 0 triage** | Decide effort level: Full / Minimal / Release |
| **First app reply** | Acknowledge the customer, create trust, support fast phone contact |
| **Call attempt** | For viable leads, the phone call is the primary qualification and conversion event |
| **Missed-call SMS** | If the call is missed, send a text immediately to keep momentum |
| **Basic qualification** | Confirm the most important next detail — do not over-question |
| **Gate 2A alignment** | Test seriousness, realism, and likely fit before locking pricing |
| **Gate 2B commitment** | Move toward approved structure, schedule, and booking |
| **Escalate if needed** | Pause and involve leadership when complexity or risk exceeds staff authority |
| **Book or release** | Every lead should intentionally move toward one of these outcomes |
| **Log and handoff** | Both booked and released leads should be documented |

---

## 7. Gate System Overview

The system uses gates to control effort, pacing, and pricing.

### Gate 0: Triage
Decides how much effort the lead deserves.

| Classification | When to Use |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Full** | Strong or viable lead worth active pursuit |
| **Minimal** | Possible fit, but not worth high-touch effort unless traction appears |
| **Release** | Bad-fit, low-margin, weak-probability, or time-wasting lead |

Gate 0 protects energy and focus.

---

### Gate 1: Contact and Basic Qualification
Focuses on: making contact, confirming urgency/timing, identifying the single most important next detail, and moving toward real conversation.

Gate 1 is intentionally light — it is not meant for full discovery.

---

### Gate 2A: Alignment
Tests whether:
- The customer is serious
- The job is real
- Expectations are reasonable
- Likely budget posture fits the work
- The job is operationally viable

When possible, Gate 2A happens on the phone.

---

### Gate 2B: Commitment
Moves the lead toward: final structure, quote framework, scheduling clarity, and booking decision.

This stage only happens after enough clarity exists to avoid sloppy commitments.

---

## 8. Call-Priority Model

The lead process is built around a **call-priority approach**. The app message and text thread support the call process — they are not the main environment for closing most viable leads.

Every lead falls into one of three call paths:

### Mandatory Call Attempt
Use when the lead is viable, valuable, urgent, or carries meaningful complexity.

**Typical examples:** moves with real scope, larger hauling jobs, same-day or next-day leads, jobs with stairs/elevators/long carry, leads where trust-building matters.

### Optional Call Attempt
Use when a call may help, but the lead may also be handled through clean text communication.

**Typical examples:** smaller simple jobs, clear-photo hauling leads, simple nearby labor tasks, leads already progressing smoothly by text.

### No Call Chase
Use when the lead is weak, low-margin, vague, unresponsive, or clearly not worth high-touch effort.

**Typical examples:** tiny far-away jobs, bargain-only leads, vague low-traction inquiries, poor-fit jobs with weak upside.

### Standard Call Sequence for Strong Leads
> App reply → call → missed-call SMS → one light follow-up if warranted → release if no traction

The system prefers calls, but does not force them when text is clearly working and the job is simple enough to stay controlled.

## 9. AI Output Structure

The AI should return each lead in a fixed internal format so the lead handler knows exactly how to use it.

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **A) Quick Read** | What kind of lead this is at a glance |
| **B) Contact Strategy** | Whether to call now, why, and what missed-call text to send if needed |
| **C) Gate Decisions** | Where the lead sits in the process and what matters next |
| **D) Next Message to Send** | The exact customer-facing message to send next |
| **E) Call Plan** | What the call is supposed to accomplish |
| **F) Branch Replies** | Likely customer responses and the exact next replies |
| **G) Pricing Posture** | Internal pricing structure guidance only |
| **H) Escalation Notes** | Whether the lead can proceed normally or should be escalated |

This format gives structure to both the AI and the staff member using it.

## 10. Escalation Philosophy

The lead handler should not freestyle risk. The system is built so the handler can own normal execution while leadership retains control over major judgment calls.

> **Standard leads can be handled within the rails. Risky leads must be escalated before commitment.**

Escalation is required when complexity grows in ways that could affect: pricing, labor, truck needs, scheduling, disposal risk, special-item handling, access difficulty, customer management, job profitability, or operational commitments.

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Name | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| **1** | Monitor | Possible issue, but still manageable for now |
| **2** | Pause before quote/commitment | Do not send pricing or make promises until approved |
| **3** | Owner takeover / direct review | Leadership needs to step in directly |

> Escalation is not failure. It is part of protecting the business.

---

## 11. Authority Boundaries

### Allowed Without Approval
- First message
- First call attempt
- Missed-call text
- Basic scope gathering
- Light follow-up
- AI-guided handling
- Simple releases
- Logging and handoff

### Not Allowed Without Escalation
- Invent complex pricing
- Discount
- Promise truck or crew without approval
- Commit same-day operations without approval
- Force certainty where major unknowns remain
- Make special handling promises on risky jobs
- Negotiate unusual customer demands outside the SOP

> These boundaries are what make delegation safe.

---

## 12. Pricing Philosophy

Pricing is one of the most protected parts of the system. Holy Hauling uses different pricing lenses depending on the service type.

### Moving = Time + Care
Key drivers: labor time, travel, stairs, elevators, long carry, tight turns, wrapping/protection, customer-controlled delays, specialty handling.

### Hauling = Volume + Disposal
Key drivers: total volume, weight, number of loads, dump fees, restricted materials, access to removal point, hidden pile growth.

**Blueprint rule:** Do not lock hard pricing before enough clarity exists.

The process may allow structure discussion, factor explanation, range logic, and alignment testing — but final commitment should only happen once the lead is sufficiently controlled.

---

## 13. Tone and Communication Standard

**Holy Hauling should sound:** warm, calm, confident, helpful, concise, and controlled.

**We want the customer to feel:** noticed, reassured, guided, less stressed, confident in our process.

**We do not want to sound:** desperate, robotic, overly salesy, defensive, chaotic, overly wordy, cheap, or pushy.

> The tone should reduce stress while still preserving control. This is where the hospitality-forward foundation begins.

---

## 14. What Hospitality Means in This System

Hospitality at intake does not mean overextending, overpromising, or doing emotional labor without boundaries.

**At this stage, hospitality means:**
- Making the customer feel noticed quickly
- Reducing confusion
- Lowering stress
- Sounding attentive and human
- Helping the next step feel easy
- Guiding the conversation calmly
- Treating the customer with care without losing structure

> This is not "softness." It is strategic reassurance.

---

## 15. What Makes This System Different

| We Do | We Do Not |
|-------|-----------|
| Qualify before committing | Throw out numbers fast |
| Gather the next most important detail | Over-question early |
| Use live conversation to build trust for viable leads | Rely on text alone |
| Protect time and focus | Chase every lead equally |
| Make the customer feel helped while staying controlled | Sound transactional |

## 16. System Summary

> Holy Hauling's lead intake system uses AI to structure new leads, prioritizes calls for viable opportunities, applies gate-based control to effort and pricing, escalates risk before overcommitting, and uses calm, hospitality-forward communication to help customers feel noticed and guided. The first message opens the door, the call does the real qualification, the gates protect the business, and every lead is intentionally moved toward booking, release, or controlled escalation.
