# Holy Hauling Escalation Contact Workflow
**Internal Use — Quick Escalation Reference**

---

## 1. Core Rule

> Do not freestyle risk. If the lead gets bigger, riskier, less clear, or more operationally sensitive, escalate **before** promising anything.

---

## 2. Escalation Levels

### Level 1 — Monitor
Keep working the lead, but stay alert.

**Use when:**
- Possible risk exists but more information may solve it
- No quote or commitment has been made yet

### Level 2 — Pause Before Quote/Commitment
Keep contact going, but do not send pricing or make promises until reviewed.

**Use when:**
- Pricing feels risky
- Access/scope is still too unclear
- Same-day pressure affects operations
- Truck/crew uncertainty exists

### Level 3 — Owner Takeover / Direct Review
Hand the strategic part up immediately.

**Use when:**
- Customer is difficult
- Job is highly complex
- Pricing risk is high
- Operational consequences are significant
- Special items or multi-load exposure are involved

---

## 3. Automatic Escalation Triggers

### Moving
Escalate if:
- 2+ flights with unclear item burden
- Elevator situation is unclear
- Long carry is significant
- Multiple stops
- Labor-only but truck timing is uncertain
- Adjustable bed, treadmill, safe, piano, or similar items
- Same-day commitment may affect schedule
- Flat-rate feels risky

### Hauling
Escalate if:
- Multiple dump runs may be needed
- Full cleanout scope is unclear
- Photos are weak or misleading
- Pile keeps growing
- Paint, tires, electronics, appliances, or other special disposal items
- Basement/upstairs/tight-access removal
- Weight burden is unclear

### Customer / Pricing
Escalate if:
- Customer pushes hard for a firm number too early
- Customer asks for discounts
- Customer wants competitor matching
- Customer keeps changing the story
- Customer is combative or unusually demanding
- AI says pause or escalate
- Your gut says the lead could go wrong

---

## 4. What You Are Allowed to Do While Escalating

### You May:
- Keep the conversation warm
- Gather basic missing details
- Clarify non-risky information
- Tell the customer you are reviewing the setup
- Log the lead and note the issue

### You May Not:
- Guess at pricing
- Promise truck or crew
- Promise same-day availability
- Offer discounts
- Imply approval has already happened
- Force certainty where uncertainty still exists

---

## 5. What to Send the Customer While Waiting

**Standard hold line:**
> *"Thanks — I'm reviewing the details so I can point you in the right direction. I'll follow up shortly."*

**More reassuring version:**
> *"Thanks, that helps. I just want to look over the setup carefully so I can guide you the right way. I'll follow up shortly."*

**When they are pressing:**
> *"I just want to make sure I'm not rushing you into the wrong direction. Let me review the setup and I'll follow up shortly."*

---

## 6. Internal Escalation Format

```
Escalation Summary

Lead type: moving / hauling
Gate stage: Gate 0 / Gate 1 / Gate 2A / Gate 2B
Customer request: [what they want right now]
Scope as currently understood: [short summary]
Access/risk: [stairs, elevator, heavy items, dump burden, etc.]
AI posture: [Full / Minimal / Release, pricing posture, escalation notes]
New issue/risk: [what changed]
Decision needed: [price / schedule / truck / release / owner takeover]
```

## 8. When to Escalate Immediately vs. Normally

### Escalate Immediately
- Same-day pressure while customer is on the phone waiting
- Truck/crew promise risk
- Special heavy item risk
- Pricing that could badly hurt margin
- Difficult customer situation

### Escalate Normally
- Moderate uncertainty
- Range confirmation
- Access clarification issues
- Quote review before sending
- Borderline release decision

---

## 9. Fast Decision Filter

Escalate if the answer to **any** of these is "yes":

- [ ] Is the job meaningfully bigger than first described?
- [ ] Is access still unclear?
- [ ] Could this quote go bad fast?
- [ ] Does this affect truck or crew planning?
- [ ] Is the customer asking for something outside the rails?
- [ ] Am I no longer confident this is inside my authority?

---

## 10. Simplest Version

> Run the lead until risk appears. When risk appears, pause before promising. Keep the customer warm. Send a clean escalation summary. Do not guess.

---

## 11. One-Line Summary

> If the lead becomes unclear, risky, operationally sensitive, or price-dangerous — escalate before committing.
