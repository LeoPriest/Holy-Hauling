# Holy Hauling Lead Handler Quick Reference
**Purpose:** Fast-use guide for handling leads in real time without reading the full SOP.

---

## 1. Core Mindset

> **AI guides. Message opens. Call converts. Gates protect. Hospitality differentiates. Escalation safeguards.**

Your job is not to chase every lead. Your job is to move good leads toward profitable booking while protecting time, pricing, and operations.

---

## 2. Lead Flow at a Glance

> Lead comes in → AI review → Gate 0 → first app reply → call if justified → missed-call SMS if needed → qualify → Gate 2A → Gate 2B → escalate if needed → book or release → log it

---

## 3. First Things First

When a lead comes in:
- Review screenshot / thread / photos
- Note timing
- Note service type
- Note scope
- Note access/risk flags
- Run viable leads through AI first

---

## 4. AI Output You Need

```
A) Quick Read
B) Contact Strategy
C) Gate Decisions
D) Next Message
E) Call Plan
F) Branch Replies
G) Pricing Posture
H) Escalation Notes
```

> Do not freestyle if the AI or situation says escalate.

---

## 5. Gate 0 Decision

| Classification | When to Use |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Full** | Strong lead — worth real effort |
| **Minimal** | Possible fit — limited effort |
| **Release** | Bad fit, weak margin, weak upside, or low odds |

*Ask yourself: Does this lead deserve a call?*

---

## 6. Call Priority

### Mandatory Call
Use for: good leads, real moves, bigger hauling jobs, urgent jobs, leads with complexity, leads where trust matters.

### Optional Call
Use for: simple smaller jobs, clear-photo jobs, text-friendly leads with low complexity.

### No Call Chase
Use for: weak leads, low-margin leads, vague poor-traction leads, bargain-only leads, jobs not worth high-touch effort.

---

## 7. Standard Sequence for Strong Leads

1. Send first app reply
2. Call
3. If no answer → send missed-call text
4. One light follow-up if warranted
5. If no traction → release

> Do not overchase.

---

## 8. Job of the First Reply

**The first message should:**
- Acknowledge the customer
- Create trust
- Unlock the phone number
- Support the call

**It should NOT:**
- Deeply qualify
- Ask too many questions
- Dump pricing
- Send a wall of text

**Example feel:**
> *"Hey [Name], thanks for reaching out — happy to help. I'm reviewing the details now and will follow up shortly."*

---

## 9. Missed-Call Text

Send **immediately** after a missed call.

**Example:**
> *"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Holy Hauling reaching out about your request on Thumbtack. Feel free to reply in the app or call/text me here — happy to help."*

Keep it: calm, clear, helpful, low pressure.

---

## 10. What the Call Is For

The call should help you:
- Build trust
- Understand real scope
- Uncover access/risk
- Test seriousness
- Reduce stress
- Frame pricing correctly
- Move toward booking

> Do not call just to "touch base." Call with a purpose.

---

## 11. What to Confirm Next

Ask only what matters next. Usually:
- Timing
- Rough scope
- Stairs/elevator/long carry
- Truck vs. labor-only
- Heavy/special items
- Rough hauling volume
- Disposal risks

> Do not interrogate the customer.

---

## 12. Gate 2A

Before locking pricing, ask:
- Is this lead serious?
- Is the scope real?
- Do expectations seem reasonable?
- Does this sound operationally viable?
- Does likely budget posture fit the work?

Whenever possible, do this **on the call**.

---

## 13. Gate 2B

Only move here when enough clarity exists. This is where you work toward:
- Final structure
- Schedule clarity
- Quote approval / commitment
- Booking

> Do not rush here just because the customer wants a number fast.

---

## 14. Pricing Reminders

### Moving = Time + Care
Think about: labor time, travel, stairs, elevator, long carry, tight turns, wrapping/protection, delays, special handling.

### Hauling = Volume + Disposal
Think about: volume, weight, dump fees, number of loads, restricted items, access, hidden pile growth.

> **Rule:** Do not lock hard pricing too early.

---

## 15. Flat-Rate Caution

Be careful with flat-rate moving if these are unclear:
- Real freight elevator or not
- Heavy/motorized items
- Wrapping status
- Truck timing control
- Likely delays

If these are unclear, do not confidently lock it alone.

---

## 16. What You Can Do Without Approval

- Review leads
- Run AI
- Send first reply
- Call
- Send missed-call SMS
- Do light follow-up
- Gather basic scope
- Confirm timing
- Handle straightforward text flow
- Release obvious bad-fit leads
- Log outcomes

---

## 17. What Must Be Escalated

Escalate if:
- Scope gets much bigger
- Multiple loads or dump runs may be needed
- Access is messy or unclear
- Stairs/elevator risk is real
- Special/heavy items are involved
- Same-day commitment affects operations
- Customer wants a hard price too early
- Discounts/custom terms are requested
- Customer is difficult
- AI output and common sense don't match

---

## 18. Escalation Levels

| Level | Name | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| **1** | Monitor | Possible issue — keep going carefully |
| **2** | Pause before quote/commitment | Do not send pricing or make promises yet |
| **3** | Owner takeover | Stop leading strategically — hand it up |

---

## 19. Hold Language While Waiting

Use:
> *"Thanks — I'm reviewing the details so I can point you in the right direction. I'll follow up shortly."*

Do **not**: guess, promise, imply approval, or give concessions to buy time.

---

## 20. Tone Standard

**Be:** warm, calm, confident, helpful, concise, controlled.

**We want the customer to feel:** noticed, reassured, guided, less stressed.

**Do not sound:** desperate, robotic, pushy, defensive, chaotic, too wordy.

---

## 21. Hospitality Reminder

**At intake, hospitality means:**
- Make them feel noticed
- Reduce confusion
- Lower stress
- Make the next step easier
- Sound attentive and human
- Keep control without being cold

**It does not mean:** overpromising, overexplaining, saying yes to everything, giving away pricing.

---

## 22. When to Release

Release when:
- Bad fit is obvious
- Margin is poor
- Job is too small for the distance
- Customer is non-responsive after allowed follow-up
- Tone suggests weak-probability bargain shopping
- The process is becoming a time sink

**Release script:**
> *"Appreciate you reaching out — based on what you're looking for, I may not be the best fit. I don't want to overpromise. Wishing you the best getting it handled."*

---

## 23. What to Log

### If Booked
- Customer name
- Service type
- Date/time
- Addresses
- Scope
- Access notes
- Price/structure
- Truck/crew notes
- Promises made
- Risks

### If Released
- Why
- Stage lost
- Notable issue

---

## 24. Fast Internal Checklist

Before sending anything, ask:
- [ ] Did I run this through AI if it's viable?
- [ ] What gate is this in?
- [ ] Does this deserve a call?
- [ ] What matters most to confirm next?
- [ ] Am I about to give pricing too early?
- [ ] Is this still inside my authority?
- [ ] Should I escalate before I promise anything?

---

## 25. Simplest Version

> Reply fast. Call good leads. Text if missed. Ask only what matters next. Do not freestyle pricing. Escalate risk. Book or release cleanly.
