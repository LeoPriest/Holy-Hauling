# Holy Hauling Onboarding & Training Plan
**Internal Use — Lead Handler Ramp Plan**

**Purpose:** Give the new lead handler a clear step-by-step path from learning the system to handling live leads with confidence and consistency.

---

## 1. What This Plan Is For

This plan answers: *How does a new lead handler go from "new to the system" to "ready to handle leads well"?*

It provides the training order, practice flow, review phases, and go-live progression. The goal is not just to teach information — the goal is to build good operational habits:

- Use AI first on viable leads
- Move fast
- Prioritize calls on strong leads
- Keep pricing controlled
- Escalate before overpromising
- Communicate in Holy Hauling tone
- Book or release cleanly

---

## 2. Training Objective

By the end of onboarding, the lead handler should be able to:

- Understand Holy Hauling's lead system
- Use the AI workspace correctly
- Classify leads using Gate 0
- Know when to call vs. when not to chase
- Send strong first replies
- Use missed-call SMS correctly
- Identify pricing posture
- Know when to hold, range, structure, or escalate
- Recognize risky leads
- Log leads consistently
- Communicate in Holy Hauling's tone

---

## 3. Training Philosophy

The best progression is:

> **Understand the system → See examples → Practice the system → Get reviewed → Handle real leads with oversight → Go live with guardrails**

That is the cleanest way to reduce early mistakes.

---

## 4. Recommended Onboarding Phases

| Phase | Focus | Goal |
|-------|-------|------|
| **Phase 1** | Learn the system | Understand what the system is, how it works, and what the role is |
| **Phase 2** | Learn the tools | AI workspace, pricing card, escalation workflow, scripts |
| **Phase 3** | Practice with sample leads | Apply Gate 0, call decisions, first messages, pricing posture, escalation |
| **Phase 4** | Shadow / reviewed live handling | Handle leads with review before anything risky is sent |
| **Phase 5** | Controlled go-live | Handle standard leads live inside authority boundaries |

## 6. Suggested Day-by-Day Ramp

### Day 1 — Orientation
**Read:** Master Index, System Blueprint

**Goal:** Understand what the system is and how the pieces fit together.

By end of Day 1, the handler should know:
- AI guides
- Calls matter
- Gates control the process
- Not all leads deserve equal effort
- Hospitality does not mean weak boundaries

---

### Day 2 — Process Training
**Read:** Lead Handler SOP, Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

**Goal:** Understand the lead flow from start to finish.

By end of Day 2, the handler should know:
- Official lead flow
- Gate 0, Gate 1, Gate 2A, Gate 2B
- Call-priority rules
- Release rules
- Logging expectations

---

### Day 3 — AI + Pricing + Escalation Tools
**Read:** AI Project Setup Pack, AI Usage Guide, Pricing Card, Escalation Contact Workflow

**Goal:** Learn how to actually operate the system.

By end of Day 3, the handler should know:
- How to submit leads to AI
- How to read the A–H output
- How to use pricing posture
- How to know when to escalate
- What not to promise

---

### Day 4 — Tone + Scripts
**Read:** Hospitality Integration Guide, Scripts Pack

**Goal:** Learn how Holy Hauling should sound.

By end of Day 4, the handler should know:
- Warm, calm, confident tone
- How to reduce customer stress
- How to keep boundaries while sounding helpful
- How to use scripts without sounding robotic

---

### Day 5 — Practice Day
Work through sample leads and provide:
- Gate 0 classification
- Call/no-call decision
- First reply
- Missed-call SMS
- Pricing posture
- Escalation decision

**Goal:** Begin translating the docs into real actions.

## 7. Practice Lead Phase

Before handling real leads alone, work through a set of sample leads.

### Minimum Practice Pack
- 3 good / viable leads
- 2 weak / release leads
- 2 escalation leads
- 2 pricing-sensitive leads

### For Each Practice Lead, Provide:
- Service type
- Gate 0 decision
- Call now / optional / no call chase
- First message
- Missed-call text
- Gate 1 next detail
- Pricing posture: hold / range / structure / lock
- Escalation decision
- One-sentence reason why

This is how you confirm the handler actually understands the system.

---

## 8. Practice Review Standard

### Good Signs
- Replies are short and calm
- AI format is used correctly
- No over-questioning early
- Calls strong leads
- Protects pricing
- Notices risk
- Escalates when appropriate
- Tone aligns with Holy Hauling

### Problems to Correct
- Too many questions
- Too much text
- Pricing too early
- Weak call instinct
- Missing risk flags
- Overconfidence on unclear jobs
- Robotic tone
- Reluctance to release weak leads

---

## 9. Shadow Phase

After practice leads, move into reviewed live handling.

### What the Handler Does:
- Runs lead through AI
- Gives Gate 0 classification
- Gives call decision
- Drafts first message
- Drafts pricing posture
- Flags escalation if needed

### What Leadership Does:
- Reviews before risky quotes or commitments
- Corrects mistakes quickly
- Explains why adjustments were made

**Goal:** Build live judgment without full independent exposure.

---

## 10. Live Review Progression

### Stage 1 — Full Review
Leadership reviews every lead classification, first message, pricing conversation, and escalation decision.

### Stage 2 — Partial Review
Handler can send: standard first replies, standard missed-call texts, simple follow-ups.
Leadership reviews: pricing-sensitive leads, risky leads, escalations, same-day leads.

### Stage 3 — Controlled Independence
Handler handles standard leads on their own inside the SOP. Escalates: risky quotes, complex moves, dump-heavy hauling, difficult customers, schedule-sensitive commitments.

---

## 11. Go-Live Readiness Checklist

Before handling leads independently, confirm:

- [ ] Understands the lead flow
- [ ] Knows when to call
- [ ] Uses AI correctly
- [ ] Understands internal vs. customer-facing output
- [ ] Uses Holy Hauling tone consistently
- [ ] Does not quote too early
- [ ] Knows pricing posture
- [ ] Knows escalation triggers
- [ ] Logs outcomes consistently
- [ ] Stays inside authority boundaries

---

## 12. First-Week Live Expectations

The goal during the first live week is **controlled consistency**, not speed alone.

The handler should aim to:
- Respond quickly
- Use AI on viable leads
- Call good leads promptly
- Send missed-call SMS immediately
- Keep first replies short
- Ask only what matters next
- Escalate before risky commitments
- Log every meaningful outcome

The handler should **not** be expected to improvise like an owner.

---

## 13. First-Month Focus Areas

By the end of the first month, improvement should be visible in:
- Reading leads faster
- Recognizing good vs. weak leads sooner
- Better call instinct
- Cleaner pricing posture decisions
- Stronger escalation judgment
- Smoother messaging tone
- Better logging discipline
- Less hesitation on obvious releases

## 15. Simple Performance Standards

The handler should consistently do these things well:
- Fast first contact
- Correct use of AI
- Correct Gate 0 choice most of the time
- Correct call choice most of the time
- No reckless pricing
- Proper escalation on risky jobs
- Clean notes/logging
- Calm and helpful tone

---

## 16. Common Early Mistakes to Expect

- Overexplaining
- Asking too many questions
- Avoiding calls
- Being too cautious on simple leads
- Being too confident on unclear leads
- Trying to price too soon
- Sounding robotic
- Not releasing weak leads fast enough
- Not re-running AI when new information changes the job

These are normal if corrected early.

---

## 17. What Success Looks Like

A successful onboarding outcome means the handler can handle standard leads in a way that is:
- Fast
- Calm
- Structured
- Profitable
- Aligned with Holy Hauling tone
- Safe within authority limits

---

## 19. One-Paragraph Version

The Holy Hauling onboarding plan is designed to move the lead handler from system understanding to controlled live handling in stages. The handler should first learn the blueprint and SOP, then learn the AI, pricing, and escalation tools, then practice on sample leads, then handle real leads under review, and only then move into controlled independence. The goal is not just knowledge, but consistent execution inside Holy Hauling's actual lead process.
