# Holy Hauling AI Usage Guide
**Internal Use — Lead Intake Operations**

**Purpose:** Teach the lead handler exactly how to use AI inside the Holy Hauling lead process without drifting from the system.

---

## 1. What This Guide Is For

This guide explains how to use AI during real lead handling. It is not a replacement for the System Blueprint, the SOP, the Quick Reference, or the Scripts Pack — it is the **bridge** between those documents and live execution.

**The goal is simple:** Use AI to help structure the lead, not to replace judgment.

---

## 2. The Role of AI in Holy Hauling

AI is used to help the lead handler:
- Understand leads quickly
- Decide whether to call now
- Know what matters next
- Generate the next message
- Prepare for the call
- Think correctly about pricing
- Identify when escalation is needed

AI is part of the process, not an optional add-on.

> **Internal rule:** For most viable leads — AI first, then action.

---

## 3. What AI Should Not Be Used For

AI should **not** be used as an excuse to:
- Freestyle pricing
- Skip escalation
- Overpromise
- Send giant paragraphs
- Avoid using judgment
- Ignore the gates
- Push weak leads too hard
- Copy internal analysis directly to the customer

> AI supports the system. It does not override the system.

---

## 4. Where AI Fits in the Lead Flow

> Lead comes in → **AI review** → Gate 0 → first app reply → call if justified → missed-call SMS if needed → qualify → Gate 2A → Gate 2B → escalate if needed → book or release → log

**Use AI at these moments:**
- A new viable lead comes in
- The customer replies with new information
- The scope changes
- Access/risk changes
- Pricing becomes relevant
- The customer objects or hesitates
- You are unsure what to send next
- You think escalation may be needed

---

## 5. When to Use AI Immediately

Run the lead through AI right away when:
- It looks like a real opportunity
- The lead is worth active effort
- The scope is not fully obvious
- A call likely makes sense
- Pricing may become a factor soon
- Access/risk may matter
- You are unsure how to classify the lead

This is the normal path.

---

## 6. When AI May Be Skipped

AI can be skipped **only** when the lead is clearly one of these:

**A. Obvious release**
- Tiny far-away job
- Clear cheapest-only lead
- Obviously bad fit
- Very weak-margin lead

**B. Owner-controlled lead**
- Owner already took over, quoted, or booked

**C. Very simple repeat/follow-up**
- Already-booked customer confirming a detail
- Known customer with a simple update
- Previously structured lead with no meaningful change

If it is not clearly one of those, use AI.

---

## 7. What to Gather Before Sending to AI

- Lead screenshot
- Any customer thread so far
- Photos if available
- Timing requested
- Service type if known
- Any known issues

Do not overthink it — just collect enough to give AI real context.

---

## 8. Standard AI Input Format

```
Holy Hauling Lead Intake Submission

Screenshot attached
Thread so far: [paste messages]
Photos: yes/no
Service type if known: moving / hauling / unknown
Requested timing: [date / time / asap]
Known issues: [stairs / elevator / special items / unknown]

Return in Holy Hauling A–H format
```

---

## 9. Official AI Output Format

```
A) Quick Read
   - Service type, timing, scope, access/risk flags, competition/tone

B) Contact Strategy
   - Call now: yes / optional / no
   - Why, contact priority, exact missed-call SMS if needed

C) Gate Decisions
   - Gate 0: Full / Minimal / Release + why
   - Gate 1: most important detail to confirm next
   - Gate 2A: yes/no on alignment + why
   - Gate 2B: what must be confirmed before quote/schedule is locked

D) Next Message to Send
   - Exact customer-facing next message

E) Call Plan
   - Call objective, top things to confirm, tone reminder, close direction

F) Branch Replies
   - Likely customer replies, exact responses

G) Pricing Posture (Internal Only)
   - Pricing structure guidance
   - Whether pricing should be discussed now or not yet
   - Key risks, approval likely needed or not

H) Escalation Notes
   - No escalation / monitor / pause / escalate now
   - Why, what trigger to watch for
```

This format is mandatory because it keeps the system controlled.

---

## 10. What Parts of the AI Output Are Customer-Facing

### Customer-Facing *(may be sent or adapted)*
- D) Next Message to Send
- Selected F) Branch Replies

### Internal Only *(never send directly)*
- A) Quick Read
- B) Contact Strategy
- C) Gate Decisions
- E) Call Plan
- G) Pricing Posture
- H) Escalation Notes

> Never paste internal analysis into the customer chat.

---

## 11. How to Read the AI Output Quickly

| Section | What It Tells You |
|---------|------------------|
| **A** | What this lead is |
| **B** | How to contact it |
| **C** | Where it sits in the system |
| **D** | What to send now |
| **E** | What to do on the call |
| **F** | What to say next if they reply |
| **G** | How to think about price |
| **H** | Whether to escalate |

---

## 12. What to Do After AI Returns the Output

**Step 1** — Read Gate 0 and Contact Strategy first.
- Is this Full, Minimal, or Release?
- Is this a call-now lead?

**Step 2** — Read Next Message to Send.
- Does this fit our tone?
- Is it low-friction?
- Does it support the call process?

**Step 3** — If the lead deserves a call, prepare using Call Plan.

**Step 4** — Check Pricing Posture and Escalation Notes before saying anything about price.

**Step 5** — Send the message, make the call if appropriate, then continue the process.

---

## 13. When to Re-Run AI

Re-run AI whenever new information changes the shape of the lead:
- Customer adds more items
- Customer changes the date
- Customer mentions stairs/elevator/long carry
- Customer says truck is needed
- Customer reveals special/heavy items
- Customer asks for pricing
- Customer pushes back on pricing
- Customer prefers text instead of a call
- Customer becomes difficult
- The job sounds more complex than first thought
- You are no longer confident in the previous output

> **Simple rule:** If the job changed, re-run AI.

---

## 14. When Not to Keep Re-Running AI

Do not re-run AI for:
- Simple "thanks"
- A basic greeting
- A confirmation that changes nothing
- Small conversational filler

Re-run AI when the lead changes **meaningfully**, not every time the customer responds.

---

## 15. How to Use AI During the Call-First Model

Because Holy Hauling prioritizes calls, AI should support the call — not replace it.

**Before the call:** Use AI to know whether to call, why, what to accomplish, and what to confirm first.

**After a missed call:** Use AI's missed-call SMS or send the approved standard version.

**After the call:** Re-run AI if major new scope came up, pricing needs structuring, risk increased, escalation may be needed, or you need the best next follow-up message.

---

## 16. AI and Pricing Discipline

**AI can help with:**
- Pricing structure and logic
- Main variables
- Whether an alignment range makes sense
- Whether to hold pricing for more clarity
- Whether approval is likely needed

**AI cannot authorize you to:**
- Invent firm complex pricing
- Discount
- Commit same-day logistics
- Promise truck or crew
- Override escalation triggers

> **Main rule:** Just because AI mentions a pricing posture does not mean you should send it. Pricing posture is internal guidance first.

---

## 17. AI and Escalation

AI can help identify risk, but it does not replace escalation judgment.

If AI says: monitor, pause before quote, or escalate now — **take that seriously.**

Also escalate when:
- The AI feels off
- The job sounds riskier than the output suggests
- The customer is pressuring you
- The lead turns complex fast
- You are unsure what promise is safe

> **Internal rule:** If AI and common sense disagree, slow down and escalate.

---

## 18. Editing AI Messages Before Sending

**You may:**
- Personalize the greeting
- Soften wording slightly
- Shorten wording
- Make it sound more natural

**You may not:**
- Add extra pricing on your own
- Add multiple unnecessary questions
- Promise availability
- Remove important caution without reason
- Turn a controlled message into a desperate one

---

## 19. Tone Check Before Sending AI-Written Messages

Before sending, ask:
- Does this sound warm?
- Does this sound calm?
- Does this sound confident?
- Is this too long?
- Is this too pushy?
- Does this reduce stress?
- Does this make the next step easier?

If not, revise slightly before sending.

---

## 20. Example AI Workflow

1. New moving lead comes in
2. Run lead through AI
3. AI says: Full, call now
4. Send first app reply
5. Call — no answer
6. Send missed-call SMS
7. Customer texts back: *"I'm on the second floor and may need truck help too"*
8. Re-run AI with new details
9. AI updates pricing posture, call plan, and escalation notes
10. Continue from there

---

## 21. Common Mistakes to Avoid

| # | Mistake |
|---|---------|
| 1 | Using AI once, then ignoring it after the lead changes |
| 2 | Copying internal analysis into customer messages |
| 3 | Sending pricing just because AI mentioned it |
| 4 | Skipping escalation because the AI output "looked good" |
| 5 | Using AI to write long robotic paragraphs |
| 6 | Not re-running AI when major complexity shows up |
| 7 | Treating AI like the decision-maker instead of a tool |

---

## 22. Daily Operating Standard

For most viable leads:
1. Gather the lead
2. Run AI
3. Read Gate 0 + Contact Strategy
4. Send first message
5. Call if appropriate
6. Text if missed
7. Re-run AI when the lead changes
8. Keep pricing controlled
9. Escalate when needed
10. Log the outcome

---

## 23. Simple Rule-of-Thumb Summary

> Use AI early. Use AI when the lead changes. Use AI to guide the next move. Do not use AI as permission to overpromise.

---

## 24. One-Paragraph Version

AI is part of Holy Hauling's lead handling system, not a replacement for judgment. For most viable leads, the lead handler should run the lead through AI before acting, use the A–H output to decide contact strategy and next steps, re-run AI whenever new information meaningfully changes the job, and treat pricing posture and escalation notes as internal guidance rather than automatic customer-facing answers. AI helps structure the work, but the lead handler is still responsible for following the gates, protecting pricing, escalating risk, and communicating in Holy Hauling's tone.
