# Holy Hauling Pricing Matrix + Modifier Framework
**Version:** Draft v1

**Purpose:** Create a more reliable internal pricing structure for Holy Hauling based on real lead patterns, transport reality, and the controlled lead-handling system.

This document works alongside the Holy Hauling lead system and intake blueprint, where moving is priced through a *time + care* lens, hard pricing is delayed until enough clarity exists, and flat rates are only earned after the main control variables are understood.

---

## 1. Why This Document Exists

Some leads were being priced incorrectly — treated like full-home moves when they were small selective moves, or like generic "truck included" moves when no moving truck is owned. This matrix helps Holy Hauling:

- Classify jobs faster
- Quote more consistently
- Protect margin
- Avoid overpricing small selective jobs
- Avoid underpricing friction-heavy jobs
- Sound more confident on the phone

---

## 2. Core Pricing Principle

**Moving = Time + Care**

We are not pricing only from mileage, Thumbtack category, customer wording, or how "small" a job sounds. We are pricing from:

- Labor time
- Access difficulty
- Truck/transport path
- Furniture care
- Setup/disassembly
- Heavy/awkward handling
- Building friction
- Coordination burden

---

## 3. The Order of Pricing

Every lead should be priced in this order:

1. **Classify** the move bucket
2. **Choose** the transport path
3. **Apply** modifiers
4. **Decide** where in the range it belongs
5. **Give** a firm number or range depending on scope clarity

> **Bucket first. Transport path second. Modifiers third. Quote last.**

---

## 4. Move Buckets

### Bucket A — Micro Carry / Single-Item

Use when the job is one couch, one mattress, one dresser, one appliance, one bed, or just one or two meaningful pieces.

**Typical examples:** couch to another apartment, washer/dryer transfer, mattress + frame delivery, same-building furniture carry

| Transport Path | Price Range |
|----------------|-------------|
| Labor only | $225–$350 |
| Pickup-truck service | $275–$450 |

---

### Bucket B — Small Selective Move

Use when the job is "just a few things," a partial apartment, 3–8 meaningful pieces, some boxes (not a full move), or one light service add-on.

**Typical examples:** bed + couch + TV + totes; desk + TVs + mirror + storage racks; partial move before the main move

| Transport Path | Price Range |
|----------------|-------------|
| Labor only | $350–$600 |
| Pickup-truck service | $450–$650 |
| Rental full service | $550–$800 |

---

### Bucket C — Standard 1BR Move

Use when the job is a true 1-bedroom move with bed, couch, dressers, TV, boxes, kitchen items — clearly more than a partial.

| Transport Path | Price Range |
|----------------|-------------|
| Labor only | $500–$700 |
| Rental full service | $650–$900 |

---

### Bucket D — Standard 2BR Move

Use when the job is a true 2-bedroom move with enough furniture and boxes to behave like a real apartment/home move.

| Transport Path | Price Range |
|----------------|-------------|
| Labor only | $650–$850 |
| Rental full service | $800–$1,100 |

---

### Bucket E — Standard 3BR+ / Full-Scale Move

Use when the job is a large home move, multi-bedroom, likely 5+ hours, with more logistics and truck dependency.

| Transport Path | Price Range |
|----------------|-------------|
| Labor only | $850–$1,150 |
| Rental full service | $1,050–$1,500+ |

---

### Bucket F — Specialty / Friction-Heavy

Use when the job includes: safes, treadmills, heavy gym equipment, TV wall mount removal, adjustable beds, sleeper sofas, uncertain elevator/truck conditions, or unusual access.

> **Rule:** Start with another bucket first, then apply specialty modifiers.

---

## 5. Transport Paths

Because Holy Hauling does not own a moving truck, pricing must reflect the actual transportation path.

### A. Labor Only
Customer rents or provides truck. Holy Hauling provides labor, loading skill, and handling.

**Best for:** apartment moves, budget-sensitive customers, keeping margin dense, avoiding rental truck coordination.

### B. Pickup-Truck Service
Holy Hauling uses its existing pickup truck(s).

**Best for:** micro carries, a few-item moves, appliance transfers, small selective jobs.

> **Important:** This is not the same as having a dedicated moving truck. Do not use this structure for full apartment/home moves unless multiple trips are intentionally built into the quote.

### C. Rental Full Service
Holy Hauling secures the rental truck and handles the move end-to-end.

**Best for:** customers who want convenience, true full-service moves, jobs where an enclosed truck is needed.

> **Important:** Always account for rental fee, mileage, fuel, truck pickup/return time, and scheduling buffer. Do not quote rental full service like a generic "truck included" move.

---

## 6. Modifiers

Modifiers answer: *What makes this job slower, riskier, or more coordination-heavy than the base bucket?*

They are not random fees. They explain why a quote sits at the low end, middle, upper end, or above the normal band.

---

### 6.1 Access / Stairs Modifier

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| Flat / easy access | +$0 |
| 1 flight | +$25–$75 |
| 2+ flights / tighter turns | +$75–$150 |
| Ugly access / long carry / bad parking / narrow stairwell | +$150–$250 |

*Affects: pace, fatigue, furniture risk, total job time.*

---

### 6.2 Distance / Zone Modifier

| Transport Path | Core Zone | Outer Zone | Far Zone |
|----------------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Labor only | +$0 | +$25–$75 | +$75–$150 |
| Pickup-truck service | +$0 | +$50–$100 | +$100–$200 |
| Rental full service | Usually built into rental cost, mileage, and fuel | | |

*Distance = dead time, fuel, wear, reduced stacking ability.*

---

### 6.3 Heavy / Awkward Item Modifier

**Examples:** treadmill, safe, sleeper sofa, recliner section, appliances, adjustable bed, solid wood dresser, oversized mirror, heavy desk, gym equipment

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| One moderate heavy item | +$50–$75 |
| One major awkward/heavy item | +$75–$150 |
| Multiple heavy/awkward items | +$150–$250+ |

*These jobs slow pace and increase handling risk.*

---

### 6.4 Assembly / Disassembly Modifier

**Examples:** bed disassembly/reassembly, table breakdown/setup, headboard/frame reassembly, furniture setup beyond standard placement

| Level | Modifier |
|-------|----------|
| Simple breakdown/setup | +$50 |
| Moderate assembly/disassembly | +$75–$125 |
| More involved setup | +$125–$200 |

---

### 6.5 Wrapping / Protection Modifier

**Included by default:** basic blankets, standard straps, ordinary careful handling, normal protection during transport.

**Not automatically included** — if you are doing *move prep* (not just move protection), wrapping should affect the quote.

**Examples of prep work:** multiple items need on-site wrapping, TVs need protection prep, mirrors/glass need padding, dressers/headboards need wrap before loading, customer is not move-ready.

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| Already wrapped / ready | +$0 |
| A couple items need extra protection prep | +$50–$100 |
| Several items need real on-site wrapping | +$100–$200 |
| Many pieces need full prep/wrapping | +$200–$300+ |

> **Internal rule:** Ask: *Am I protecting the move, or am I preparing the move?* If preparing, wrapping moves the quote up.

---

### 6.6 Truck Coordination Modifier

**Examples:** customer has not secured truck yet, Holy Hauling must rent truck, truck pickup/return eats time, truck timing is uncertain, customer-controlled truck timing is sloppy.

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| Customer has truck ready / controlled | +$0 |
| Truck timing slightly uncertain | +$25–$75 |
| Rental truck required on our side | +actual cost + buffer |
| Truck coordination is a headache | +$75–$150 beyond rental reality |

*Truck chaos destroys margin.*

---

### 6.7 Elevator / Building Logistics Modifier

**Examples:** passenger elevator instead of freight, elevator reservations, long apartment hallways, loading dock issues, downtown parking/building friction, multi-door apartment paths.

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| Simple elevator / easy building | +$0–$50 |
| Passenger elevator / moderate friction | +$50–$100 |
| Freight uncertainty / long hallway / loading issues | +$100–$200 |

*Elevator jobs often look easy on paper but run long in real life.*

---

### 6.8 Fragile / High-Care Item Modifier

**Examples:** large TVs, mirrors, art, glass tables, delicate electronics, heirloom furniture.

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| One or two fragile items | Lean to mid/high end of range |
| Several fragile / high-care items | +$50–$150 |
| Fragile-heavy job | +$150–$250 |

---

### 6.9 Urgency / Short-Notice Modifier

**Examples:** same-day, next-day, customer says urgent, schedule must be rearranged.

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| No urgency | +$0 |
| Next-day / schedule squeeze | +$50–$100 |
| Same-day / real urgency | +$100–$200 |

*Urgency affects your calendar, not just the job itself.*

---

### 6.10 Scope Confidence Modifier *(Internal Only)*

| Scope Clarity | Pricing Posture |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Clear scope | Firm number okay |
| One missing detail | Small range okay |
| Several swing factors | Stay with a range until clarified |

---

## 7. How to Apply Modifiers

Do not stack modifiers mindlessly like a calculator. Modifiers help decide whether the quote belongs at the low end, middle, upper end, or above the band.

**Shortcut rule:**
- 1 friction point → low-middle
- 2 friction points → middle
- 3+ friction points → upper end
- Rental truck + multiple friction points → above middle quickly

---

## 8. Live Use Format

For every quote, work through:

```
A. Bucket
   Micro / Small Selective / Standard 1BR / Standard 2BR / Standard 3BR / Specialty

B. Transport Path
   Labor only / Pickup-truck service / Rental full service

C. Modifier Check
   □ Access / Stairs
   □ Distance
   □ Truck coordination
   □ Heavy items
   □ Assembly
   □ Wrapping
   □ Elevator / building
   □ Fragile items
   □ Urgency
   □ Scope confidence

D. Position in Range
   Low end / Middle / Upper end / Above band (explain why)
```

## 9. Example Applications

### Example A — Single couch, local, one flight, pickup-truck service
- Bucket: Micro carry
- Transport path: Pickup-truck service
- Modifiers: one flight, standard couch, local
- **Quote: $225–$275 if intentionally competitive, otherwise $275–$325**

### Example B — Bed + couch section + TV + totes, rental truck required
- Bucket: Small selective
- Transport path: Rental full service
- Modifiers: local-ish, one flight, light item count
- **Quote: $625–$700**

### Example C — Full 2BR, under 5 miles, 1 flight at unload
- Bucket: Standard 2BR
- Transport path: Rental full service or labor only
- Modifiers: one flight, standard furniture
- **Quote: $800–$875 rental full service**

### Example D — Desk + TVs + mirror + boxes + TV mount removal
- Bucket: Small selective
- Transport path: Labor only or truck included
- Modifiers: distance, fragile items, TV mount removal
- **Quote: $395–$525 labor only; $495–$625 with truck path**

### Example E — In-building move with treadmill, TVs, mirrors, bed setup, passenger elevator
- Bucket: Small selective / heavy in-building move
- Transport path: Labor only
- Modifiers: elevator friction, treadmill, fragile items, assembly
- **Quote: Upper-middle of the band**

## 10. Guardrails

| Rule | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Do not price from miles alone | Distance modifies the quote — it does not define it |
| Do not price from Thumbtack's category alone | A "1 bedroom" lead can be partial, full, or a few selective items |
| Do not price every small selective move like a whole-home move | This has been one of the biggest corrections needed |
| Do not ignore truck path | Because Holy Hauling does not own a moving truck, transport path must be explicit |
| Do not discount because a zip code feels price-sensitive | Instead, offer labor-only option or smaller-scope path — this protects pricing integrity |

## 11. Internal Summary

> Classify the move. Choose the transport path. Check the friction points. Then decide where in the band it belongs.
