# Holy Hauling Lead Handler SOP
**Version:** Internal Working SOP

**Purpose:** Train a lead handler to manage incoming leads using Holy Hauling's AI-assisted, call-priority system while protecting pricing, schedule, and customer experience.

---

## 1. Purpose of the Role

The lead handler's job is not to chase every lead or book every possible job. The job is to help Holy Hauling book **profitable, controlled, realistic work** by:

- Responding quickly
- Using AI before acting on viable leads
- Prioritizing calls where appropriate
- Gathering the right details at the right time
- Following the gate system
- Escalating complexity before it becomes a bad quote or bad promise
- Moving leads toward booking, release, or controlled handoff

> A released lead is not always a failure. If a lead is a bad fit, releasing it early is a successful outcome.

---

## 2. Core Operating Model

> **AI guides. Message opens. Call converts. Gates protect. Hospitality differentiates. Escalation safeguards.**

Holy Hauling uses an AI-guided, call-priority, hospitality-forward lead system with gate-based control. That means:
- AI helps structure the lead before action
- The first message opens the door
- The call is the main qualification and conversion tool for viable leads
- Gates control effort, pacing, and pricing
- Escalation protects the business
- Tone should make customers feel helped, reassured, and guided

---

## 3. Success Standard

Success is measured by:
- Fast contact
- More meaningful live conversations
- Better-qualified opportunities
- Profitable bookings
- Clean release of weak leads
- Fewer pricing mistakes
- Fewer operational surprises

---

## 4. Required System Flow

> Lead comes in → AI review → Gate 0 triage → first app reply → call attempt when justified → missed-call SMS if needed → contact/basic qualification → Gate 2A alignment → Gate 2B commitment → escalate if needed → book or release → log and handoff

---

## 5. Non-Negotiable Operating Rules

### Rule 1: Most Viable Leads Go Through AI First
Before replying to a viable lead, run it through the Holy Hauling AI format.

**Exceptions:**
- Obvious release leads
- Leads already fully controlled by owner
- Direct follow-up from an already-booked or already-quoted customer

### Rule 2: The First Message Is Not for Deep Qualification
The first app reply should: acknowledge the lead, create trust, unlock the phone number, and support a fast call attempt. It should **not** try to solve the whole lead in one message.

### Rule 3: Calls Are the Main Tool for Viable Leads
For good leads, the phone call is the main place to build trust, clarify scope, surface risk, test seriousness, frame pricing, and move toward booking.

### Rule 4: Do Not Lock Hard Pricing Too Early
Do not give a firm quote before enough clarity exists on scope, access, timing, truck/labor setup, and disposal burden (if hauling).

### Rule 5: Do Not Freestyle Risk
If the job becomes complex, risky, unclear, or operationally sensitive, escalate.

---

## 6. Lead Arrival Procedure

When a new lead comes in, gather what is available:
- Lead screenshot
- Customer request details
- Thread history
- Photos if provided
- Requested date/timing
- Any obvious access or item notes

Do a fast read — not a deep analysis. At this stage, prepare the lead for AI and triage.

---

## 7. AI-First Procedure

### Standard Input Format
```
Lead Intake Submission

Screenshot attached
Thread so far: [paste messages]
Photos: yes/no
Service type if known: moving / hauling / unknown
Requested timing: [date/time/asap]
Known issues: [stairs / elevator / special items / unknown]

Return in Holy Hauling A–H format
```

### Official AI Output Format

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **A) Quick Read** | What the lead is at a glance |
| **B) Contact Strategy** | Whether to call now, why, and what missed-call text to send |
| **C) Gate Decisions** | Gate 0, Gate 1, Gate 2A, Gate 2B guidance |
| **D) Next Message to Send** | Exact customer-facing next message |
| **E) Call Plan** | What the call is supposed to accomplish |
| **F) Branch Replies** | Likely customer replies and exact next responses |
| **G) Pricing Posture** | Internal pricing guidance only |
| **H) Escalation Notes** | Whether to proceed, monitor, pause, or escalate |

This format is mandatory because it keeps lead handling consistent.

---

## 8. Gate 0 — Triage

Gate 0 happens immediately after the lead is reviewed. Its purpose is to decide how much effort the lead deserves before time is invested.

### Full
Use for viable leads worth active pursuit.
- Meaningful move or worthwhile haul-out
- Time-sensitive but realistic lead
- Real revenue potential
- A call could meaningfully improve close chance

### Minimal
Use for possible fits that are not worth high-touch effort yet.
- Vague small job
- Lower-margin opportunity
- Unclear lead with some possible upside
- Simple lead where one message may be enough to test viability

### Release
Use for poor-fit leads or low-value leads not worth chasing.
- Cheapest-only tone
- Tiny job far away
- Obvious low-margin lead
- Low-probability or chaotic lead

> **Gate 0 rule:** Not every lead deserves the same effort. That is part of the system, not a failure of the system.

---

## 9. Call-Priority Rules

Every lead falls into one of three call paths.

### A. Mandatory Call Attempt
Call after the first app reply when the lead is viable, valuable, urgent, complex, or likely to benefit from trust-building.

**Typical examples:** apartment/house moves, multi-item moves, larger hauling jobs, time-sensitive leads, leads with possible access issues, leads where details are still light but opportunity is real.

### B. Optional Call Attempt
Call may help, but text-only handling is acceptable.

**Typical examples:** smaller simple jobs, clear-photo hauling leads, nearby low-complexity tasks, leads already moving smoothly by text.

### C. No Call Chase
Do not actively pursue by phone.

**Typical examples:** tiny far-away jobs, weak-margin leads, poor-traction leads, bargain-only leads, low-upside vague inquiries.

### Standard Sequence for Strong Leads
> App reply → call → missed-call SMS → one light follow-up if warranted → release if no traction

---

## 10. First App Reply Procedure

For Full and most Minimal leads, send the first app reply quickly.

**Job of the first reply:** Acknowledge the customer, sound helpful and responsive, create trust, unlock the phone number, support the call attempt.

**What not to do:** Do not deeply qualify, ask too many questions, send pricing too early, or send a wall of text.

**Example first-reply tone:**
> *"Hey [Name], thanks for reaching out — happy to help. I'm reviewing the details now and will follow up shortly."*

You may use the AI-generated message as long as it matches Holy Hauling tone.

---

## 11. Call Procedure

For mandatory-call leads, place the call soon after the first app reply.

### Purpose of the Call
- Build trust
- Make the customer feel helped
- Clarify real scope
- Uncover access/risk
- Test seriousness
- Reduce stress and confusion
- Move toward pricing and booking if appropriate

### Main Call Objectives

**Moving — confirm:**
- Item count / major pieces
- Stairs, elevator, long carry, tight turns
- Labor-only vs. truck-needed
- Timing window
- Handling/protection needs
- Whether the job is simple enough to structure

**Hauling — confirm:**
- Total volume
- Heavy items
- Special disposal items
- Access to the junk
- Likely number of loads or dump-fee exposure
- Urgency and fit

**Tone on the call:** Be calm, warm, confident, helpful, concise, and directional. The customer should feel like you know how to make the process easier.

---

## 12. Missed-Call SMS Procedure

If the customer does not answer, send a text right away.

**Purpose:** Keep momentum alive, identify who you are, reference the request, make it easy to continue by app/text/phone, avoid sounding pushy.

**Example:**
> *"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Holy Hauling reaching out about your request on Thumbtack. Feel free to reply in the app or call/text me here — happy to help."*

---

## 13. Light Follow-Up Rules

The goal of follow-up is to test for traction and keep the process moving efficiently — not to chase forever.

### For Strong Leads
- First app reply
- First call attempt
- Missed-call SMS
- One light follow-up if warranted

### For Weaker Leads
- First app reply
- Optional call
- Optional missed-call SMS
- One light follow-up max

### Stop Chasing When
- The value is weak
- The traction is weak
- The lead becomes a time sink
- The customer goes silent after the allowed sequence

---

## 14. Gate 1 — Contact and Basic Qualification

Gate 1 exists to: make contact, confirm timing, confirm baseline scope, and identify the most important next detail. It is intentionally light.

**What to confirm next** — usually only the single most important detail, such as:
- Whether timing is flexible
- Whether the job is labor-only or truck-needed
- Rough number of large items
- Whether stairs/elevator matter
- Rough junk volume
- Whether special disposal items exist

> **Question discipline:** Do not ask five stacked questions. Ask what matters next.

---

## 15. Gate 2A — Alignment

Gate 2A checks whether the lead is aligned before a firm price is locked. It may use ranges or process framing, but it is **not** the stage for a fully locked quote.

### What You Are Testing
- Is the customer serious?
- Does the job sound real?
- Are expectations reasonable?
- Does likely budget posture fit the work?
- Is the job operationally viable?

**Rules:**
- Whenever possible, Gate 2A should happen on the call
- One follow-up is allowed after Gate 2A
- Do not change pricing in that follow-up
- If misaligned, release quickly and professionally

---

## 16. Gate 2B — Commitment

Only leads that pass Gate 2A move to Gate 2B. This is where price structure, timing, and commitment are locked.

### Typical Structures

**Moving:**
- Hourly + minimum
- Flat rate with guardrail
- Flat rate + wait-time clause

**Hauling:**
- Tiered volume pricing
- Range first, firm number after clarification
- Dump fees separate or included up to a cap with approval rules

Do not move into hard commitment until enough clarity exists.

---

## 17. Pricing Philosophy

### Moving = Time + Care
Main drivers: labor time, travel, stairs, elevators, long carry, tight turns, wrapping/protection, delays, specialty handling

### Hauling = Volume + Disposal
Main drivers: volume, weight, dump fees, restricted items, number of trips, access to removal point, hidden pile growth

**Main rule:** Do not lock hard pricing before enough clarity exists.

---

## 18. Flat-Rate Caution Rules

Flat-rate moving should only be used when control is strong enough. Before confidently locking a flat rate, confirm:

- True freight elevator vs. passenger-style elevator
- Whether any motorized or extra-heavy items exist
- Whether wrapping is already done or needs a clause
- Who controls the truck timing
- Whether delays are likely

If these are not clear, do not confidently lock a flat rate alone.

---

## 19. Authority Boundaries

### Allowed Without Approval
- Review leads
- Run AI
- Send first app reply
- Call
- Send missed-call SMS
- Continue light follow-up
- Gather basic scope
- Confirm timing
- Identify obvious access concerns
- Continue straightforward text handling
- Release obvious bad-fit leads
- Log outcomes
- Hand off booked jobs

### Not Allowed Without Escalation
- Invent complex pricing
- Offer discounts
- Promise crew size
- Promise truck availability
- Promise same-day operations
- Override serious risk flags
- Promise specialty-item handling
- Make questionable disposal promises
- Negotiate unusual customer demands
- Force certainty where big unknowns remain

> The lead handler runs the system. Leadership owns risky judgment calls.

---

## 20. Escalation Rules

### Escalate When:
- Scope becomes much larger than expected
- Multiple dump runs may be required
- Access sounds messy or high-risk
- Stairs/elevator details are unclear
- Heavy or special items are involved
- Same-day commitment may affect operations
- The customer pushes hard for a firm price too early
- Discounts or custom terms are requested
- The customer is combative or unusually difficult
- AI output and common sense do not match

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Name | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | **Monitor** | Possible issue exists, but continue handling for now |
| 2 | **Pause before quote/commitment** | Do not send pricing or commit until approved |
| 3 | **Owner takeover / direct review** | Leadership needs to step in directly |

### Escalation Format
```
Escalation Summary

Lead type:
Gate stage:
Customer request:
New issue/risk:
AI posture:
What I need decided:
```

---

## 21. Hold Language While Waiting on Escalation

Use holding language like:
> *"Thanks — I'm reviewing the details so I can point you in the right direction. I'll follow up shortly."*

Do **not**: promise a number, promise availability, imply approval already exists, or offer concessions to keep the lead warm.

---

## 22. Tone Standard

**Holy Hauling should sound:** warm, calm, confident, helpful, concise, and controlled.

**We want customers to feel:** noticed, reassured, guided, less stressed, confident in our process.

**We do not want to sound:** desperate, robotic, overly salesy, defensive, chaotic, overly wordy, cheap, or pushy.

> *Be warm enough to feel human. Be calm enough to feel safe. Be confident enough to feel capable. Be clear enough to move the job forward.*

---

## 23. Hospitality-Forward Intake Standard

**At intake, hospitality means:**
- Making the customer feel noticed quickly
- Reducing confusion
- Lowering stress
- Sounding attentive and human
- Making the next step feel easier
- Guiding calmly without losing structure

**It does not mean:**
- Overpromising
- Overexplaining
- Overaccommodating
- Giving away pricing
- Saying yes to everything

> Hospitality is strategic reassurance, not loose boundaries.

---

## 24. Release Procedure

Release a lead when:
- It is clearly a bad fit
- The margin is poor
- The customer is non-responsive after the allowed sequence
- The lead is too small relative to distance
- The tone strongly suggests poor-fit bargain shopping
- The SOP clearly supports release

**Release script:**
> *"Appreciate you reaching out — based on what you're looking for, I may not be the best fit. I don't want to overpromise. Wishing you the best getting it handled."*

---

## 25. Logging and Handoff

### For Booked Leads, Capture:
- Customer name
- Service type
- Date/time
- Addresses
- Scope summary
- Access notes
- Price/structure
- Payment method if known
- Crew/truck notes
- Special risk notes
- Any promises made

### For Released Leads, Capture:
- Reason for release
- Stage where lead died
- Notable issue if relevant

Once a lead is booked, move it to the job tracker and hand it off cleanly.

---

## 26. Daily Rule-of-Thumb Summary

1. Run viable leads through AI first
2. Reply fast
3. Use the first message to open the door, not solve the whole job
4. Call good leads quickly
5. Text immediately if the call is missed
6. Ask only what matters next
7. Use Gate 2A before hard commitment
8. Do not freestyle pricing
9. Escalate risk
10. Book, release, or hand off cleanly

---

## 27. One-Paragraph Version

Your role is to run Holy Hauling's lead system with speed, discipline, and good judgment. Use AI to structure viable leads, use the first message to make contact, use calls as the main qualification tool, keep pricing controlled until enough clarity exists, escalate complexity before promising anything risky, and communicate in a warm, calm, confident way that makes customers feel helped without losing boundaries.
