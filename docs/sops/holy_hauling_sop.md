## Services We Offer
- **Moving**: residential and commercial moves — quoted by crew size × hours + truck + materials
- **Hauling**: junk removal, cleanouts — quoted by load volume + disposal fees
- Moving and hauling use different pricing models — never mix them

## Gate Workflow
- **Gate 0 — Triage**: Is this lead viable? Confirm service type, rough location, basic timeline.
- **Gate 1 — Contact & Qualify**: First call within 1 hour. Verify customer is real and serious.
- **Gate 2A — Alignment**: Confirm scope, date, crew needs, any access issues.
- **Gate 2B — Commitment**: Customer agrees to terms; ready to book.
- **Outcome**: Book / Release / Escalate

## Contact Protocol
- Call first — always. Text is only a follow-up tool.
- Voicemail: leave a brief, professional message; follow immediately with text.
- No answer after 3 attempts → escalate or release depending on lead quality.

## Release Criteria (releasing is a valid, healthy outcome)
- Location outside our service area
- Timeline we cannot accommodate
- Scope outside our service types
- Budget gap too large to close

## Escalation Criteria
- Job scope is unusually large or unclear
- Customer is hostile, unresponsive, or making unreasonable demands
- Pricing dispute beyond facilitator authority
- Any legal, liability, or safety concern

## Pricing Posture — INTERNAL ONLY, DO NOT SHARE WITH CUSTOMER
- Moving baseline: 2-person crew × 3 hrs + truck = ~$450; add for stairs, long carry, extra crew
- Hauling baseline: quarter load ~$200 / half ~$350 / full ~$550 + disposal fees
- Quote confidence: **high** = clear scope, **medium** = some assumptions made, **low** = needs on-site assessment
- Never quote firm price over phone for complex jobs; offer estimate range only
