How do users identify themselves?

This shapes the entire role system. Pick the approach that fits how your team actually works.

A

Simple role picker — no password

On launch, the app asks "Who are you?" — Admin, Facilitator, or Field Crew. No accounts, no login. Role stored locally. Anyone can switch if they know what they're doing.

Pros

  • Zero friction
  • No account management
  • Works offline immediately

Cons

  • No real access control
  • Anyone can switch to Admin
B

PIN per role — lightweight protection

Admin and Facilitator require a PIN set in Settings. Field Crew has no PIN. Entering the wrong PIN keeps you at Field Crew level. PIN stored in app settings (not per-user).

Pros

  • Protects sensitive data
  • No account setup per person
  • Admin can reset PINs

Cons

  • Shared PIN (anyone who knows it)
  • No per-person audit trail
C

Named user accounts — full identity

Each person has a username + PIN/password and is assigned a role. Admin can add/remove users. Audit trail shows who did what by name. Most complex to build.

Pros

  • True per-person audit trail
  • Role changes require admin
  • Scales to larger teams

Cons

  • Most build effort
  • Account management overhead
